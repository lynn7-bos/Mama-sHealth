Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do to prepare for pregnancy?

Begin by scheduling a preconception check-up with your healthcare provider. Adopt a healthy lifestyle: eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly, and avoid smoking or alcohol. Take prenatal vitamins with folic acid to reduce the risk of birth defects..

What are the warning signs during pregnancy that need immediate medical attention?

Seek immediate medical care if you experience: Severe abdominal pain or cramping, Heavy bleeding or unusual discharge, Persistent headaches, vision changes, or swelling in the face or hands and Decreased fetal movement.

How do I know if I’m in labor?

Signs of labor include: Regular, strong contractions that get closer together over time. Water breaking (amniotic sac rupture). Lower back pain and pressure. If unsure, contact your healthcare provider or visit a hospital.

How can I manage postpartum depression?

Recognize the signs: feelings of sadness, fatigue, or disinterest in the baby lasting more than two weeks. Reach out to your doctor for support or therapy. Join support groups to share experiences with other mothers.

What should I expect in the first weeks after childbirth?

Your body will be healing, so rest and good nutrition are essential. Breastfeeding might take time to adjust, and lactation consultants can help. Schedule a postpartum check-up 6 weeks after delivery. Keep track of the baby’s vaccinations and health milestones.

What vaccinations does my newborn need?

At birth, your baby should receive the following vaccinations: BCG (to prevent tuberculosis). Hepatitis B. Oral polio vaccine (as per your region's health guidelines). Your doctor will guide you on the immunization schedule.
